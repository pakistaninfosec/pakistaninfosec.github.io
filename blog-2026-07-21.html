As we navigate the ever-evolving landscape of cybersecurity, it's becoming increasingly clear that the threats we face are not only becoming more sophisticated but also more widespread. Today, our threat monitors have picked up a staggering 167,383 threats, with 559 new ones emerging just in the last 24 hours. Of particular concern are the 78 zero-day exploits and 28 actively exploited vulnerabilities that have been identified. For Pakistani organizations, the situation is just as dire, with 15 specific alerts issued by the Pakistan CERT, highlighting critical vulnerabilities in popular products like Fortinet FortiSandbox and Palo Alto Networks GlobalProtect. In this digest, we'll delve into the key threats making headlines today and explore what they mean for our local cybersecurity landscape.


Critical SharePoint RCE Under Active Exploitation

One of the most alarming stories to emerge in recent days is the active exploitation of CVE-2026-50522, a critical deserialization of untrusted data vulnerability in Microsoft Office SharePoint. This flaw, which carries a CVSS score of 9.8, could allow unauthorized attackers to execute code over a network, posing a significant risk to organizations that rely on SharePoint for collaboration and content management. Given the widespread use of SharePoint in sectors like banking, telecom, and government, this vulnerability has the potential to cause considerable disruption in Pakistan. Microsoft has already patched this vulnerability as part of its July 2026 Patch Tuesday update, but the fact that it's under active exploitation underscores the importance of prompt patching and robust security measures.


WordPress Flaws Exploited for Webshells

Another critical vulnerability suite that's been making waves is the "wp2shell" flaws (CVE-2026-63030 and CVE-2026-60137) affecting WordPress Core. These vulnerabilities are being exploited by hackers to deploy persistent webshells and install malicious plugins on affected servers. Given the popularity of WordPress among Pakistani websites, including those in the healthcare and e-commerce sectors, it's essential for local organizations to ensure their WordPress installations are up to date and that any plugins or themes are thoroughly vetted for security. The exploitation of these flaws highlights the ongoing challenge of securing open-source software and the need for vigilance in maintaining the security of web applications.


Closing Identity Gaps in Critical Infrastructure

A recent article by Specops Software emphasizes the critical role of identity verification in securing critical infrastructure. The piece notes that many attacks on critical infrastructure begin with stolen credentials, compromised devices, or trusted accounts, underscoring the importance of implementing a Zero Trust approach that verifies both user identities and device trust before granting access to sensitive systems. This advice is particularly relevant for Pakistani organizations in sectors like energy, finance, and government, where the consequences of a security breach could be catastrophic. By adopting a Zero Trust model, these organizations can significantly reduce their attack surface and better protect themselves against the evolving threat landscape.


Pakistan Impact

The threats outlined above have significant implications for Pakistani organizations across various sectors. The active exploitation of SharePoint and WordPress vulnerabilities, for example, could lead to data breaches, intellectual property theft, and disruption of critical services. Moreover, the emphasis on securing identity and access in critical infrastructure highlights the need for Pakistani organizations to review their current security practices and ensure they are aligned with best practices. This includes implementing robust identity and access management solutions, regularly updating software and systems, and conducting thorough security audits to identify and mitigate potential vulnerabilities.


Actionable Recommendations

So, what can Pakistani security teams do today to protect themselves against these threats? Here are a few concrete actions:

  1. Patch Immediately: Ensure that all systems, especially those running Microsoft Office SharePoint and WordPress, are updated with the latest security patches.
  2. Implement Zero Trust: Begin implementing a Zero Trust security model that verifies the identity of users and the trustworthiness of devices before granting access to critical systems and data.
  3. Conduct Security Audits: Perform thorough security audits to identify potential vulnerabilities in web applications, networks, and critical infrastructure, and develop plans to mitigate these risks.
  4. Enhance Monitoring: Enhance monitoring capabilities to quickly detect and respond to potential security incidents, including the exploitation of zero-day vulnerabilities and unauthorized access attempts.
  5. Educate Users: Educate users about the importance of cybersecurity and the role they play in preventing breaches, including the use of strong passwords, avoidance of suspicious links, and reporting of unusual activity.

    6. Closing

    In conclusion, the cybersecurity landscape is more challenging than ever, with threats evolving at a pace that can seem overwhelming. However, by staying informed, taking proactive measures, and prioritizing security, Pakistani organizations can significantly reduce their risk exposure. Today's threats are a stark reminder of the importance of vigilance and the need for a comprehensive security strategy that encompasses patching, identity verification, and user education. As we move forward in this complex environment, it's crucial that we remain adaptable, proactive, and committed to protecting our digital assets. Only through such a concerted effort can we hope to mitigate the impact of these threats and build a more secure digital future for Pakistan.
