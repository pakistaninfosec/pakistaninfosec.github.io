Frequently Asked Questions
Is InfoSec Pakistan free to use? ▼
Yes, InfoSec Pakistan is completely free. Our mission is to democratize cybersecurity intelligence for Pakistani organizations, security professionals, and government agencies.
How do I subscribe to the daily email digest? ▼
Send us an email at pakistaninfosec@gmail.com with subject "Subscribe" and your email address. You'll receive our daily threat intelligence digest every morning at 09:00 PKT with color-coded severity alerts, AI analysis, and Pakistan CERT advisories.
How do I report a vulnerability or security incident? ▼
Email us at pakistaninfosec@gmail.com with subject "Vulnerability Report". For national incidents, also report to Pakistan CERT at pkcert.gov.pk and NCCS Pakistan at nccs.pk. Please include CVE ID if available, affected systems, and severity assessment.
How often is the threat data updated? ▼
Our automated pipeline runs every day at 09:00 PKT. It scrapes 5 verified threat intelligence sources, generates an AI-powered executive summary using Groq, and updates the website and email digest simultaneously.
Can organizations partner or collaborate with InfoSec Pakistan? ▼
Absolutely! We welcome collaboration with Pakistani universities, government agencies, cybersecurity companies, and research institutions. Email us at pakistaninfosec@gmail.com with subject "Collaboration" to discuss partnership opportunities.
What sources does InfoSec Pakistan monitor? ▼
We monitor 5 verified sources: CISA KEV (Known Exploited Vulnerabilities), NVD/NIST (National Vulnerability Database), Exploit-DB (public exploit archive), Pakistan CERT (pkcert.gov.pk), and NCCS Pakistan (nccs.pk). More sources are being added continuously.
